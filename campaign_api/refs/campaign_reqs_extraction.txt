[Title] Bar Campaign
[] The BAR campaign is a 49-mission, single continuous story spanning four factions: Tutorial, Cortex, Armada, and Legion, each structured into three narrative arcs. The campaign evolves from small-scale survival to full galactic conflict, culminating in a fragile three-way balance of power.
[Heading2] Structure Overview
[] Total Missions: 52
[] Tutorial Campaign: 4 missions (1 Narrative Arc)
[] Cortex Campaign: 16 missions (3 Narrative Arcs)
[] Armada Campaign: 16 missions (3 Narrative Arcs)
[] Legion Campaign: 16 missions (3 Narrative Arcs)
[] Release Schedule
[] Demo includes Tutorial Campaign + First Cortex Arc (8 Missions)
[] 1.0 Includes Tutorial, Cortex, and Armada Campaigns (36 Missions)
[] ?? Includes Legion Campaign (52 Missions)
[Heading2] Narrative Overview
[Heading3] Tutorial Campaign (Independent)
[] The player begins as a salvager on the Line World Cetheta, discovering a lost Cortex transport carrying Tenebrium and eventually escaping. Introduces the player to:
[] Core mechanics
[] Commanders and planetary warfare
[] The Cortex and Armada conflict
[] This arc is primarily worldbuilding, but also establishes key characters and sets up future Legion storylines.
[Heading3] Cortex Campaign 
[Heading4] Arc 1: A Mantle Earned
[] The player survives an ambush, rebuilds a Commander chassis, defeats an Armada incursion, and becomes a commander. Early mysteries emerge around Scavernite and Keramite research.
[Heading4] Arc 2: The Raptor War
[] Deployed to the Line Worlds, the player fights a losing war against Raptors, rescuing civilians and uncovering Armada’s experiments that created the Raptors, creating a desperate situation for Cortex civilization.
[Heading4] Arc 3: The Forbidden Threshold
[] Desperate, the player defies Cortex law to resurrect the Prime All-Father AI using a combination of Tenebirum, Scavernite, and an All-Father Memory Core. The attempt fails catastrophically, unleashing the Scavenger Menace.
[Heading3] Armada Campaign
[Heading4] Arc 1: Suppressing Independence
[] Amid rising instability in the Nomadic Reaches, the player crushes independent uprisings and some Cortex Raiders while skirmishing against a few scattered Scavenger and Raptor threats.
[Heading4] Arc 2: The Scavenger Menace
[] Scavs emerge as a dominant force, overwhelming both factions. Armada is forced into defense, making strategic sacrifices. Although they try to deploy Raptors against Scavengers unsuccessfully, and focus on saving planets in the path of the scavengers.
[Heading4] Arc 3: Alliance Against Extinction
[] The corrupted All-Father unifies the Scavs into a galaxy-wide threat.Armada, Cortex, and independent forces form a tenuous (non - faction wide) alliance to destroy it. Victory is achieved, but trust between factions remains fractured.
[Heading3] Legion Campaign
[Heading4] Arc 1: The Five Clans
[] In the aftermath of the Scavengers, The player unifies five independent rival clans through diplomacy and conquest, uniting them into a single new faction, “Legion”.
[Heading4] Arc 2: Securing the Frontier
[] Legion expands into the Terminal Verge, reclaiming lost worlds and positioning itself as a stabilizing force against lingering Scavs and Raptors.
[Heading4] Arc 3: The Third Power Rises
[] Legion challenges Armada, securing its place as a major faction. 
[Heading2] Narrative Summary
[] 3 Narrative Arcs per Faction Campaign, with each arc composed of 5 missions, for 9 “Chapters” in the story.

[Heading3] Legion Campaign Arcs
[Heading4] 1st Legion Arc
[] Clan 5 emerges from the aftermath of the Corrupted All-Father’s destruction with confidence and desired legitimacy for true independence. However, they remain fragmented, divided into rival clans with overlapping claims, histories, and beliefs. The player rises as a unifying force, traveling between scattered territories to bring the clans together under a single banner. Each mission is against a different clan, some join willingly, others must be defeated. Each Clan unlocks their roster of units. Player can select the order to attack different clans and enable their units. By the end of the arc, the player has unified all five clans, forming Legion.
[Heading4] 2nd Legion Arc
[] With the Legion unified, the player turns back towards Nullspace and the factions. They push into the Terminal Verge, contesting Cortex positions and securing Legion territory as Cortex rebuilds from the Scavenger War. They defend/restore isolated colonies abandoned/still fighting against Scavs and Raptors. Some are taken by force, others through rescue as Legion tries to present itself as a protector rather than an occupier. As builders of stability in regions where Cortex order has collapsed. Some of the raptors are different, more powerful, void type.
[Heading4] 3rd Legion Arc
[] With Cortex targets of opportunity realized, Legion begins its conflict with Armada. The player pushes into the Nomadic Reaches, seizing mobile strongholds and forcing Armada forces into retreat. With momentum secured, Legion advances toward the Line Worlds, where Cortex and Armada interests overlap and neither is willing to cede ground. To push Legion as a fully realized third power, the player defeats multiple commanders from each side in a final battle. Victory is bittersweet however, as a new raptor evolution has hit, void raptors. The player has 2 missions, one to track the new void queen that could potentially FTL raptors across space, and a second to actually hunt and kill her. The final mission are allies from Cortex and Armada coming together to kill the new void raptor queen. Her death kills attach void raptors, similar to how NCS units die with their commander.

[Heading1] Tutorial Campaign 
[] Introduces the player to the BAR universe as a young independent, part of a salvaging crew roaming Line World Cetheta. After discovering a missing Cortex transport full of Tenebrium, the crew make a frantic escape back to their ship to get offworld. The player learns basic mechanics and controls, and introduces lore anchors like Tenebrium, the void, Commanders, Terraforming, etc. The conflict frames the clash of two titans with the independents scurrying around the exterior.  
[Heading2] Characters
[Heading3] The Pale Harbor
[] The Pale Harbor is an independent long-range hauler refitted for scavenging runs along the Lineworlds. Its crew of three generally operate outside faction contracts, running between conflict zones to reclaim derelict tech, schematics, and Tenebrium from abandoned battlefields.
[] Gunmetal, with red highlights.  Cortex bulk, but some sleek elements to denote that it can speed away.  Needs a custom model, large cargo bay.  Name is written in Cortex on one side of the hull and Legion/Independent on the other. 4 seater, but they never take a 4th member, the empty seat remembers those who are missing.  Minor weaponry, but can’t contest serious ships.
[] The Pale Harbor’s transponder has been scrubbed repeatedly, and has multiple fake registrars including, “Ashen Meridian”, “Silver Streak”, “Vergeborn”, and “Unaligned Labor Vessel 04.”  
[Heading3] Kaiyro Vlo (Captain of the Pale Harbor)
[] Captain Kaiyro Vlo, born in the Terminal Verge, grew up among the husks of independent cities and shattered stations left behind by the brutality of the Severance War. His early years were spent surviving the collapse of Independent space under the retaliation of Cortex and Armada in the aftermath of the Sundering., he also had to survive the corsair flotillas roaming independent space. 
[] Kaiyro gained his salvaging and piloting skills from this upbringing, navigating through irradiated debris belts and derelict shipyards where even the lights of the stars were drowned by dust and static. He learned to live by instinct within the silence, skills essential to surviving the pirate flotillas and scavenger syndicates that prowled the Verge in search of the last vestiges of Tenebrium. In those years of drifting through Nullspace, he changed. Like many Verge-born survivors, he is void touched, with a disquieting awareness that sets him apart. Having seen the fate of enough independent void-touched, he keeps his change hidden from the factions.
[] His status as Verge-born denies him entry into inner Cortex territory, which suits him fine. He has seen the efficiency of the machine firsthand and wants no part of it. Yet his heritage grants him access to Cortex Lineworlds, where only Verge crews can maneuver through unstable terrain and decaying infrastructure. There, amid the ruin, he and his crew thrive.
[] Balance between anger over the Severance War and its damage to independents, and how the independents took from the faction during the Sundering, and fought/raided amongst themselves instead of being unified.  Wolves at every door.  Individualist.
[] Dislike of the control that comes from the chain and mantle, but also is drawn to the stability and safety of it, contrasts well against his rough upbringing. He can appreciate it. Despite chances to integrate further into Cortex Society he has remained independent, refusing to commit loyalty to anyone or any cause
[] Cybernetics - something survivally, eye / hand most likely. Like the concept of him losing a limb during his youth. Also a number of scars. Loves noodles. Charismatic
[] Tone is low, deliberate, controlled. Demeanor is calm under pressure, flashes of dry humor, pragmatic with a touch of fatalism. Jaded idealist, reluctant leader, loyal to individuals but distrustful of systems.
[] “Every wreck has a story, but they’re wrecks all the same.”“Keep the engines warm. I never trust quiet skies.”“Funny thing about history, someone always thinks they’re the good part.”“You can tell how long a war’s been going by how polite the wreckage looks.”“You hear that pitch change? [Heading3] Lira Daedub (Navigator and Engineer)
[] Lira Daedub, the ship’s navigator and engineer, is a former Armada freight technician who deserted during the Severance escalation after witnessing the slaughter of an unarmed transport convoy containing no tenebrium or rebels. Her defection is against the continuum council and the great houses, not to Armada culture itself. She still maintains a horizon journal and has several gene slices. Lira’s technical precision and quiet authority keep the ship’s Tenebrium shielding stable and its engines functional long past safe tolerances. She often clashes with Kaiyro’s improvisational instincts, yet the two share a bond forged through decades of narrow escapes. In encounters with Armada patrols, Lira often “takes over” the captaincy, leveraging her old codes and clipped officer’s diction to bluff safe passage.
[] Long enhanced fingernails, like colored talons, which is why she doesn’t pilot anything. Flirty and dynamic. Enjoys scolding, and red starscapes. Whenever she does engineering work, she uses custom control mitts to move smaller robot hands, so her nails don’t get messed up.Warm tone but sharp; clear diction with emotional range. Quick and agile, alternates between banter and bursts of focus. Confident, teasing, often scolds but rarely cruel.“If I wanted to die for a cause, I’d have picked one that feeds me better.”“You patch the hull, I’ll patch the story. One of them has to hold.”“Every star looks red from far enough away. Maybe that’s why I like them.”
[] Should be the emotional balance to Kaiyro’s stoicism.

[Heading3] Vessel Subroutine Fourteen (V-14)
[] Their shipboard AI, “Vessel Subroutine Fourteen” (V-14), was once a Keram’s Horizon logistics node before its data core was recovered from a Cortex derelict. Although operating at reduced cognition, it manages maintenance and sensor routines and occasionally blurts fragmented recitations of Echelon Code and Keram’s Philosophy (looooves AI/robots) in a distorted cadence. Its remaining logic chains still insist on classifying the crew by outdated Cortex ranks, which the humans have long since stopped correcting.

[Heading3] Planet Cetheta
[] The surface is a shattered desert, a cracked expanse of scorched clay and rusted alloys stretching for hundreds of kilometers.  Between the glassed terrain and silicate ash, the natural biome of Cetheta has long since vanished; what remains is a ruin continually rewritten by the factions’ competing terraforming towers. 
[] As the battleline crawls across the planet, each faction reshapes the land behind it Cortex -&gt; Humid tropical lowlands, Armada -&gt; Temperate forests and wind swept plains. Between these opposing climates is the battlefield/no-man’s land.  From orbit, Cetheta’s surface appears pocked by colors. Deserts darkened with blast marks and war damage, with pockets and stretches of green and blue around the terraforming towers.
[] The Armada/Cortex war has persisted on Cetheta for almost sixty local years. Neither side holds lasting control; instead, they advance and withdraw in endless mechanical tides. Cortex has three commanders, Taran Lexhold, Serev Vorn, and Arandon Senoble

[Heading2] TM1: Salvage Rights
[] The Pale Harbor drops into low flight over the Line World of Cetheta, weaving through mountains and trenches to stay clear of a massive Cortex/Armada engagement to the north. Kaiyro and Lira navigate through interference as V-14 tracks overlapping signatures, keeping them hidden at the battle’s edge. After landing in a crater and handing controls to the kid, the crew pushes south along a ruined trench scarred with decades of wreckage. But as they search for salvage, the battlefront above collapses toward them, forcing the Pale Harbor to dive deeper into the trench to avoid detection.
[] Cut off from the ship, Kaiyro pushes forward alone and discovers a shattered Apoc-S12 transport filled with refined Tenebrium. Despite the danger and distance, he commits to taking it. Inside the wreck, they find a constructor chassis, and V-14 hijacks the dormant machines nearby, giving the kid their first controllable units. With enemy forces closing in, Kaiyro and the kid haul the Tenebrium out of the trench and flee east as the collapsing front sweeps over their trail. The theft does not go unnoticed, and pursuit begins immediately.
[Heading2] TM2: Mantle Over the Mountainside
[] Still hauling the stolen Tenebrium, the convoy pushes east across the canyon floor as Cortex comms spike and patrols tighten their pursuit. Lira reports that lockdown broadcasts have gone out and Commander Senoble has been warned. Forced forward, Kaiyro and the kid cross a landslide and push through a lightly defended Cortex outpost.
[] Descending the mountainside, they gain additional forces and are forced to avoid a fortified chokepoint lined with LLTs. They collect brutes and take an alternate route, through hostile patrols and the LLT chokepoint, where they break through with the brutes.  As they try to leave, Commander Senoble decloaks and Dguns the front brutes. With no hope of fighting a commander, Kaiyro orders a full retreat. TM2 ends with the Independents outrunning Senoble on flat ground, driving the stolen Tenebrium deeper towards the Pale Harbor.
[Heading2] TM3: Where the Chain Runs Thin
[] Shaken but still fleeing, the Tenebrium convoy continues along a ridge paralleling the road, but find the route merging directly into a heavily fortified Cortex garrison sealing the canyon ahead. With no way around, they must break through. Scouting the outskirts, they uncover damaged but intact infrastructure, two Vehicle Plants and a Bot Lab that V-14 is able to reactivate, and he feeds every produced Rascal and Incisor into the kid’s growing force. Despite minor skirmishes with reclaimers outside the garrison’s outer walls, the kid is able to defeat the outlying forces.
[] Across the valley is a bot lab, which is restored, and produces grunts. Once the garrison’s defenses crumble, Kaiyro moves the hauler through the shattered perimeter, carving open a corridor deeper into Cortex space. TM3 ends with the Independents smashing through the blockade and pushing east, but closing in on the Pale Horizon but still hunted and outmatched.
[Heading2] TM4: Pale Harbor Rising
[] The convoy finally reaches the Pale Harbor, but the stolen Tenebrium is now bleeding a detectable signal across most of Cetheta. As Kaiyro races back from the ridge, Lira clamps the hauler in place and deploys constructors, leaving the kid to establish a defensive line against whatever is closing in. The kid builds a base and fortifies the southern valleys against the approaching enemies.
[] Cortex forces strike first, testing the new defenses while the Pale Harbor crew begins loading the Tenebrium and plotting their jump path. As the ship’s engines warm through their cycles, Armada units arrive next, followed in the final stretch by Divinitas Mandate forces intent on reclaiming what was stolen. The kid holds the line long enough for the Pale Harbor to finish preparations. Engines ignited and hold sealed, the ship blasts skyward under fire. TM4 ends the tutorial with the Independents escaping Cetheta, now fugitives from both Cortex and Armada.

[Heading2] Themes, messages, goals.
[] The CENTRAL foundation-myth, the raison d’etre, of Cortex Society is the sustenance of civilization. The ensured survival and expansion of humanity. It is the positive promise of continuity and a perennial flowering of purpose in life, in community with others. It has its positive prescription in its structures, in its reverences, in its love for humanity, love for high civilization, and desire to see it spread across the stars. ALL OF THIS has been GIVEN through the All-Father, to whose stewardship we owe the current preservation and expansion of mankind, but the next step of the narrative is disputed by the subfactions of Cortex.
[] To it, the ultimate good is the adherence to civilization in its codes, the reverence for our forebears, the call of responsibility to your community and those around you, the control of the excesses of human emotion and the tendency of human nature to indolence like in the Pax Dormancy.
[] To it, the ultimate evil is “All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way,” like Armada that has turned everyone to his own way. It is to reject your duty, it is the self-obsessed, selfish individualism that marks the destruction of civilization like the actions of the old Earth elites that sparked the Mortalis Rebellion.
[] Loyalty to Structure/Echelon Code vs Loyalty to the Spirit of The Law
[] The Horizon vs Traditionalism
[] Accountability vs Mercy (I have an end-of-campaign scene in mind). High Conclave’s role as high-level mediator between the chaos of life and the Code.
[] Pushing humans beyond their nature (or the inability to do so)
[] The nature of unchosen (and chosen) obligations based on place in society (aka The Great Chain of Being)
[] (re)Forging The Chain
[] The Place of AI, and the place of Faith, in a functioning futuristic society
[] A traditionalist vision of the future, at least compared to say Star Trek (which is imo already traditionalist in some unexpected ways)
[] The Mantle as Nobless Oblige, and The Chain and The Echelon Code as Accountability
[] Meritocracy of The Chain, skin in the game, ‘earning’ status, and the failures of corruption that bypass these things, painting a picture which will ‘oppose’ Armada which is both more corrupt but also more ruthlessly meritocratic in a technocratic sense.
[] “War makes orphans of us all” - and Found Family (because it is a highly beloved modern trope as traditional senses of family have crumbled)
[] Man’s Fallen Nature (not in the Hobbesian state-of-nature sense, but in the more ancient and ‘theological’ sense), and the cultural structures of Cortex that attempt to bind and elevate them.
[] Heroic Sacrifice: NO SENSELESS DEATHS
[] Konstantin Gawain and Harmony Folke: for their breach, some of the promises of Cortex Society are taken away from them. (The promise of immortality via Memory)
[] Harmony Folke: NOT a leader “by her nature” so she doesn’t even want to try NCS. Maybe some people try to force/pressure her?

[Heading3] Konstantin Gawain
[] Main Player Character. Mantleguard, grandson of Dekellan Gawain. Becomes Commander.
[] — Background + Beliefs: He grew up on the Perseus Gate, and watched the God-Killer Basilisks rebuff the Armadan fleet. He is Mantleguard, as were half the people on Perseus Gate, but lived in a Covenant-controlled territory. His youth saw the crises that rocked Cortex during the early Severance War, the Echelon Schism incidents, and the near failure of the Cortex war Machine until Veylos Keram won the day, and shaped him into someone who can understand where the Divinitas Mandate is coming from. As such, while traditional Mantleguard interpretations of the Echelon Code and The Chain hold importance to him, he values the High Conclave and its unifying deliberation higher than any individual subfaction, cultural pillar, or alignment.
[] Character Arc: Coming to grips with sudden command post. Balancing between his Mantleguard instincts/reverence of the Echelon Code, and the increasingly desperate situation for Cortex: choosing his own chain, going against his grandfather.
[Heading3] Chorial: Dekellan Gawain, Margrave of Perseus Gate, and High Mantlebearer
[] — Background + Beliefs: A voice of morality, practical clarity to the Echelon Code, and tactics. Dekellan was born OT-3497. His life started as the heir apparent to a Cortex-aligned territory on a colony, and battled crises and division to claim his mantle once his father passed. That colony became a core world of Cortex just before the Tenebrium Age truly kicked off. When Cortex proper came offering reintegration, he jumped at the chance. Shaped by the lessons inflicted on himself and his people during his ascendancy, he deeply values the Echelon Code’s steadiness and moral character, which made him an immediate Mantleguard officer. He died in service as an old man, during the Severance War, and now lives on as a Chorial to advise his grandson the Player Character.
[] — Physical description: ‘Pluto’ face shape, the ‘loyal warrior,’ that is tall and strong. Downturned (negative canthal tilt) eyes that contrast with the strength of the rest of his appearance, importantly reflecting the sensitive and caring nature of his rule. And/or go for a Philosopher-King archetype for looks. Head up, shoulders back. Heavier armor. Some other thing that indicates his rulership rather than armor, maybe a circlet or a writ/paper document attached somewhere.
[Heading3] Harmony Folke, the Duty-Veiled, The Maid of Mandate
[] — Background: Like her mother, who wove transcendentally ennobling tapestries for Garmethdes public spaces, Folke was destined to be an artist touched by the divine. But, like many Garmethdes believers who were content to live humble lives in service to the community they belonged to, the scars torn through the stars by Armada’s callous warfare woke her from the dream she was immersed in. In that moment of lucidity, finding her home reduced to ash, she nearly went mad, but seized the determination to punish Armada and to set the galaxy aright. She quickly found a new place in the Chain, as a prodigy pilot whose human impulse overrode AI suggestions to spectacularly inspiring results. She was quickly promoted to an officer rank, but she has little desire to command soldiers as it is not in her nature, so it seems more like an honorary title. Rather, she acts as a rallying cry, an inspiration to the Cortex warriors around her.
[] — Personality and views: Driven, passionate, occasionally reflects the humble transcendental solemnity of her folk artistic youth, charismatic in the way of translating emotion to word and deed, and inflaming the hearts of warriors around her. Ultimately, respects the authority of her superiors in The Chain as the natural and good order of things, and will, on occasion, struggle to submit the passionate drive to their decisions. She hates Armada and views them as amoral monsters who seek to destroy everything that she holds dear. The death of her father - a man she considered wise in his authority - and the occasional counsel of his Chorial, convinced her that the Garmethdes Covenant was entirely correct in seeking the return of the All-Father. She believes in Cortex as a whole, and can articulate the spirit of it with a simple emotive passion, from the ground up UNTIL the High Conclave, where the belief in the All-Father as the rightful ruler of Cortex takes over her dialogue. Compassionate on a similar wavelength as Dekellan Gawain, but on a more base and personal level rather than his structural/leadership one.
[] — Physical description for arty bois: A very feminine (cute) jawline/face shape with an upturned chin. A prominent ‘button’ nose (as in, little nasal bone). Large ‘hunter’ eyes, straight and strong eyebrows, and full lips that reflect her assertive emotional intelligence and convicted drive. Long, straight black hair but with a ‘rugged’ perpetual bedhead, and a bandana with Garmethdes symbolism (tapestry, hieroglyphs) on it that helps keep her bangs controlled in a middle part, and covers her hugeass forehead.
[] — “message”: it’s ok to not be a girlboss. Character arc: submitting with humility to a choice that the Player Character makes, which they initially object to, potentially the final decision by Commander Gawain to bring them both before the High Conclave.
[Heading3] Kancho Umikinu, Operative Golden Ocean
[] — The Keramite high ranks are trifurcated between secular-minded individuals who simply seek more flexibility in pursuing their goals, true adherents to the Transhumanist Ascension narrative that is the public face of Keram’s Horizon’s theological impulse, and moderate, theologically-inclined intellectuals carved from Path of the Eternal Horizon believers who are either sympathetic to or can’t quite bring themselves to believe the religious narrative of the Garmethdes Covenant (and thereby prefer the Keramite interpretation by default). Operative Umikinu, intimately connected to the higher ranks as a briefing officer, found himself between the secular-theological divide of the high ranks.
[] — Background + Beliefs: Keram’s Horizon, impressed by the mission logs from the Obligate Weight and Cireth Gammad, seek something of their own Raptors. Operative “Golden Ocean” is a ~200 year old intelligence officer, and, at the time he is introduced into the campaign, field operative on a mission to spread Scavernite into new environments. During the Mantleguard Infinite Divisor Order infighting following the founding of Keram’s Horizon, he was swayed by Veylos Keram’s belief that The Chain, and especially the Echelon Code, was too rigid, and risks shattering Cortex as a whole. The infighting left him personally disfigured, and he is heavily augmented, something like a living embodiment of the Keramites striving towards cybernetic integration, and so appears mostly human (until half of his face is taken off). Now, as a field operative, he views himself as an agent of the greater good: willing to break the rules to do what must be done for the survival of Cortex.
[] — Campaign function and pivotal intent: He functions first as an intelligent foil to the Mantleguard Chorial Margrave Dekellan’s moralizing sentiment, and gives the player (character) a window into galaxy politics and characters by drawing on his past as an intelligence briefing officer. It is this disposition which causes him to be annoyed at Garmethdes-Chan’s suggestions that they ought to resurrect the All-Father, but he realizes he can use the opportunity to give agency to Scavernite and will eventually direct the party to resurrect the All-Father so that he can create the Scavengers proper. As an experienced intelligence officer, he likely knows (or could intimate) something about Raptors being an Armadan creation + Armada ‘controlled’.
[] — Character arc(s): Being ‘exposed’ by Abe Bakerite does a number on his psyche, which he resolves by the end of arc 1 by embracing Keramite transhumanism. Might influence his action to rez the All-Father with Scavernite.
